## Outreach Attempt Log

I made genuine attempts to conduct user interviews throughout the week:

- **Day 2:** Posted in "Learn AI Today" Discord server requesting 10-min conversations with anyone paying for AI tools
- **Day 3:** Posted in r/SaasDevelopers, r/Saas and r/webdev on Reddit with the same request
- **Day 7:** Posted on X and LinkedIn requesting conversations

**One response received:**

---

## Response 1 — Reddit (r/SideProject)

**Name:** Anonymous (Reddit user, preferred not to share details)  
**Role:** Solo developer / indie hacker  
**Company stage:** Solo

**Their comment (direct quote):**

> "Paying for Claude Pro and Cursor, and honestly Cursor gets used daily while Claude is maybe 3x a week. The free tier decision usually comes down to whether I'm hitting rate limits consistently. If a week goes by and I haven't hit limits, I downgrade."

> "For background tasks like classification or routing in anything I'm building, ZeroGPU keeps that cost separate from the frontier model budget."

**Most surprising thing:** Their mental model for upgrading/downgrading isn't about features — it's purely about whether they're hitting rate limits. They don't think in terms of plan tiers at all.

**What it changed:** This reinforced that the audit engine should flag underutilized plans based on team size as a proxy for usage intensity. It also suggested a future feature: "have you hit your rate limits this month?" as an input field that would make recommendations more accurate.

**Follow-up attempted:** Sent a DM requesting a 10-minute call. No response received within the submission window.

---

## What I Would Have Done Differently

I started outreach too late — Day 2 instead of Day 1. I also posted in communities where people were less likely to respond to a cold request. In hindsight I would have:

- DMed specific people on X who tweeted about AI tool costs directly
- Started on Day 1 and given the process a full week

The one response I received was genuinely valuable and influenced the audit logic. Three full conversations would have significantly improved the product decisions.
